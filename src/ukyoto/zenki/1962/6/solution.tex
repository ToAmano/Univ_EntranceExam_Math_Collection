［解］$y = 4 - x^2$ 上の $x = t$ での接線 $l$ は ($0 < t < 2$)
\[
l: y = f(x) = -2tx + 4 + t^2
\]
だからこれが $(a, 0)$ を通る時，
\[
0 = t^2 - 2at + 4
\]
\[
\therefore t = a - \sqrt{a^2 - 4} \quad (\because t < 2, a > 2)
\]
である。これを $t_0$ とおくと，右図から

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.8]
  \draw[->] (-0.5,0) -- (3,0) node[right] {$x$};
  \draw[->] (0,-0.5) -- (0,3) node[above] {$y$};

  \draw[thick] (0.5, 3.75) -- (2.5, -0.25) node[above right] {カタムキ $-2t_0$};
  \fill[pattern=north east lines] (1, 3) -- (2, 0) -- (1, 0) -- cycle;
  \node[above right] at (1.5, 0.8) {$S$};
  \node[below] at (1, 0) {$t_0$};
  \node[below] at (2, 0) {$2$};
  \node[below] at (2.5, 0) {$a$};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

求める面積 $S$ は
\begin{align*}
S &= \int_{t_0}^2 (f(x) - (4-x^2)) dx + \frac{1}{2} (a-2)^2 \cdot 2t_0 \\
&= \int_{t_0}^2 (x - t_0)^2 dx + t_0 (a-2)^2 \\
&= \frac{1}{3} (2 - t_0)^3 + t_0 (a-2)^2
\end{align*}
（$A = \sqrt{2+a}, B = \sqrt{a-2}$ とおく）
\begin{align*}
&= \frac{1}{3} (-B^2 + AB)^3 + (a - AB) B^4 \\
&= \frac{1}{3} B^3 (A^3 - 3A^2 B + 3AB^2 - B^3) + aB^4 - AB^5 \\
&= \frac{1}{3} B^3 (A^3 - B^3) - A^2 B^4 + aB^4 \\
&= \frac{1}{3} \left( (a^2-4)^\frac{3}{2} - (a-2)^3 \right) - 2(a-2)^2 \\
&= \frac{1}{3} (a^2-4)^\frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{3} (a+4)(a-2)^2
\end{align*}
